KМ555ТМ2 / SN74LS74A-compatible dual D flip-flop
===================================================

Primary reference
-----------------
Texas Instruments SDLS119, SN54LS74A/SN74LS74A dual positive-edge-triggered
D-type flip-flops with preset and clear, December 1983, revised March 1988.
Local file: sn74ls74a-ti.pdf

14-pin package contract
-----------------------
1   1CLR_N   asynchronous active-low clear
2   1D       synchronous data
3   1CLK     positive-edge clock
4   1PRE_N   asynchronous active-low preset
5   1Q       true output
6   1Q_N     complementary output
7   GND
8   2Q_N     complementary output
9   2Q       true output
10  2PRE_N   asynchronous active-low preset
11  2CLK     positive-edge clock
12  2D       synchronous data
13  2CLR_N   asynchronous active-low clear
14  VCC

Functional contract
-------------------
- CLR_N low and PRE_N high forces Q=0 and Q_N=1.
- PRE_N low and CLR_N high forces Q=1 and Q_N=0.
- With both asynchronous controls high, a rising CLK edge transfers D to Q.
- With both asynchronous controls high and no rising edge, the prior state is
  retained.
- Simultaneously asserting CLR_N and PRE_N is outside normal complementary-
  output operation and must not be used as a deterministic board state.

D96 board-specific constraint
-----------------------------
Recovered .009 sheet 3 ties D96.10 2PRE_N and D96.12 2D to the same
FDC_IRQ_CONDITIONED_N node, while D96.13 2CLR_N is drawn unconnected. This
wiring cannot drive 2Q low through any documented deterministic control:
condition low asynchronously presets Q; condition high makes D=1, so a rising
clock also sets or preserves Q. Treat an unconnected LS-TTL input as a
hardware-quality/measurement issue, not as a guaranteed logic-high source.
Consequently D96 section 2 remains a reconstruction contradiction until the
pin-13 disposition and pins 9/11 remote endpoints are verified on hardware.

D96 section 1 ties 1CLR_N/pin1 and 1PRE_N/pin4 together at WREQ_N. WREQ_N low
therefore invokes the datasheet's simultaneous-assertion row, with both Q1 and
Q1_N high rather than a clear-priority reset. Simultaneous release does not
guarantee restart phase. Q1_N feedback still produces divide-by-two behavior
after a valid clock resolves the state, but deterministic write-time reset and
restart claims are not supported by the primary device contract.

Board truth remains in kicad/juku.board.json and the exact-revision sheet/photo
evidence. This note constrains device behavior; it does not invent a missing
copper connection.
